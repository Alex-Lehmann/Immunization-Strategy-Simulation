Our model requires an estimate of the infection and spread potential of COVID-19. We chose to use the effective reproduction number \(R_e\), which accounts for time-dependent variations in transmissibility1. This can include such factors as government intervention measures, social distancing, and others that may provide a more comprehensive picture of virus transmissibility in the provionce.

The Government of Ontario has, since 16 November 2020, published a daily estimate of the \(R_e\) of the COVID-19 virus in Ontario, specifically2. This data forms the basis for the transmissibility parameter of the virus in our model. The provincial data begins on 2020-03-13 and ends on 2021-02-02, a range which includes all four seasons and a wide variety of levels of pandemic control measures. We reasoned that on the large scale and long timeline of our simulation, this data could likely be used to obtain a reasonable estimate of \(R_e\) going forward.
